What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

Small Data Files

Cookies store small amounts of information like preferences, login status, and browsing behavior.

Temporary or Persistent

Some cookies expire when you close your browser, while others remain on your device for longer.

Safe & Secure

Cookies cannot access other files on your device or install malware. They only store information you provide.

You're in Control

You can manage, block, or delete cookies through your browser settings at any time.

Managing Your Cookies

You have full control over cookies. You can manage, block, or delete cookies through your browser settings or our cookie preferences tool:

Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. Here's how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

Google Chrome

  1. Click the three dots menu (⋮)
  2. Go to Settings → Privacy and security
  3. Click "Cookies and other site data"
  4. Choose your preferred cookie settings

Mozilla Firefox

  1. Click the menu button (≡)
  2. Go to Settings → Privacy & Security
  3. Under "Cookies and Site Data"
  4. Adjust your cookie preferences

Safari

  1. Click Safari menu → Preferences
  2. Go to the Privacy tab
  3. Choose "Block all cookies" or customize
  4. Click "Manage Website Data" to view/delete

Microsoft Edge

  1. Click the three dots menu (…)
  2. Go to Settings → Cookies and site permissions
  3. Click "Cookies and site data"
  4. Manage or clear cookies

Rezvo Cookie Preferences

You can also manage your cookie preferences directly on Rezvo by clicking the "Manage Preferences" button in our cookie banner or visiting your account settings.

Important Note

Blocking or deleting certain cookies may impact your experience on Rezvo. Essential cookies are required for the platform to function properly and cannot be disabled. Analytics and marketing cookies can be disabled without affecting core functionality.
